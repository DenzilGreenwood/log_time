The LTQG Framework

Mathematical Foundation

Core Transformation

The fundamental LTQG transformation maps proper time τ to logarithmic time σ:

σ = log(τ/τ₀) ⟺ τ = τ₀ exp(σ)

This maps the semi-infinite interval (0, ∞) to the entire real line (-∞, ∞), with singularities at τ = 0 corresponding to σ → -∞.

Modified Schrödinger Equation

Quantum evolution in σ-time follows:

iℏ ∂|ψ⟩/∂σ = K(σ)|ψ⟩ = τ₀ exp(σ) H|ψ⟩

The generator K(σ) exhibits asymptotic silence: K(σ) → 0 as σ → -∞, naturally regularizing quantum evolution near spacetime singularities.

Gravitational Redshift Unification

Multiplicative time dilation becomes additive in σ-coordinates:

τ_B = α τ_A ⟹ σ_B = σ_A + log(α)

This enables natural incorporation of gravitational effects into quantum phase evolution without fundamental modifications to either theory.
